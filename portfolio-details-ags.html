Case Study

AGS Logistics

An early hybrid role across internal software, website management, and digital marketing.

At AGS Logistics, I worked across both technical and marketing functions. My role included developing and maintaining an in-house ERP system for internal operations, managing the company website, improving UI and usability, supporting SEO and social media activity, and creating brand and marketing materials. It was an early role that helped shape my ability to work across code, systems, websites, and digital growth.

Role
Web Application Developer | Digital Marketing
Business
AGS Logistics Global, Dubai
Focus

Technical + marketing hybrid role
What changed
AGS benefited from stronger internal system support, a more functional and user-friendly website, and an active digital presence supported by both technical execution and marketing output.

Internal systems and external brand touchpoints

Goal

The starting point

A logistics business needed support across both operations and digital visibility

This role sat at the intersection of software development and digital marketing. On one side, the business relied on internal systems for daily logistics work. On the other, it needed a website, digital presence, and marketing materials that presented the company professionally.

The challenge

AGS required hands-on support across multiple layers of the business. Internal workflows depended on a custom system that needed maintenance and improvement, while the company’s website and digital channels also needed consistent technical and marketing attention.

Core responsibilities

  • Develop and maintain an in-house ERP system
  • Support daily technical issues and operational continuity
  • Manage and improve the company website
  • Plan and execute SEO, content, and social media efforts
  • Create marketing and brand collateral for the business
  • Improve usability across internal and public-facing systems
The priority: support smoother internal workflows while strengthening the company’s digital presence through better systems, websites, and marketing output.
My role

Application development, website management, and digital marketing under one function

This was a broad execution role where I worked across backend systems, frontend UI, database logic, website administration, and digital marketing support. It gave me early exposure to how technical infrastructure and business visibility can connect inside one organisation.

Technical responsibilities

  • Developed and maintained the in-house ERP system
  • Worked with C#, ASP.NET, JavaScript, and SQL
  • Optimised database performance and built stored procedures
  • Resolved day-to-day technical issues affecting operations

Digital and creative responsibilities

  • Managed and maintained the company website in WordPress
  • Handled SEO, content, and social media support
  • Created brand and marketing materials for the business
  • Applied UI/UX thinking to improve usability and presentation
What I did

A hybrid scope across systems, websites, and brand support

The role covered internal development work as well as external-facing digital responsibilities. That combination made it one of the earliest experiences that shaped my hybrid skill set.

In-house ERP development

Developed and maintained the company’s internal ERP system used to support daily logistics operations.

  • Worked in C#, ASP.NET MVC, JavaScript, and MSSQL
  • Handled coding, debugging, and system improvements
  • Supported operational continuity through daily technical fixes
  • Helped make internal workflows more dependable and efficient

Database and backend optimisation

Improved performance and data handling through backend logic and SQL work.

  • Optimised database queries and performance
  • Developed stored procedures for data retrieval
  • Supported smoother system behaviour
  • Worked across the full software development lifecycle

Website development and management

Managed the company website in WordPress with a focus on usability, responsiveness, and relevance.

  • Developed and maintained the corporate website
  • Applied SEO basics and content improvements
  • Improved UX through responsive design and cleaner structure
  • Handled hosting and website administration support

Frontend UI and UX

Applied frontend design thinking across systems and website interfaces to make them easier to use.

  • Worked on user-friendly UI layouts
  • Improved usability across internal and external platforms
  • Focused on responsive and practical design
  • Balanced functionality with clarity

Digital marketing and social media

Supported AGS’s digital presence through SEO, content, and social media activity.

  • Planned and executed basic digital marketing initiatives
  • Created and managed social media accounts
  • Published content to improve brand visibility
  • Supported a more active online presence for the company

Creative and brand support

Created practical marketing materials used in everyday business communication and branding.

  • Designed flyers, brochures, and business cards
  • Created basic brand and promotional visuals
  • Supported offline and online communication needs
  • Combined technical work with hands-on creative execution
Impact

What this work changed

AGS Logistics gained stronger support across its internal systems and digital presence through one role that covered technical reliability, website upkeep, and basic marketing execution.

Better operational support

Internal teams benefited from ongoing ERP support, issue resolution, and system maintenance tied to daily logistics work.

Stronger digital presence

The business had a more functional website, active social channels, and better digital support for visibility and communication.

Broader execution capability

This role demonstrated the value of combining development, UI/UX, website management, and marketing under one skill set.

Most importantly: AGS Logistics helped shape my foundation as someone who can move between systems, websites, and digital marketing depending on what the business needs.

Key takeaways

What this project says about how I work

  • I can work across both internal systems and customer-facing digital platforms.
  • I bring together development, UI/UX, website management, and marketing support in one workflow.
  • I am comfortable solving practical business problems, not just isolated technical tasks.
  • This role laid the foundation for the hybrid digital skill set I use today.
